ABOUT INDRIYA

        INDRIYA is a State level Project Development competition conducted by IEEE MEC SB in association with the Women in Engineering Affinity Group for students from classes 8th to 12th. We aim to improve the students’ skill in ideation, while mentoring and guiding them along every step of the way, in order to help their innovative ideas take shape.

INDRIYA is a Project Development Competition introduced by IEEE MEC SB in 2018.INDRIYA is the perfect platform for students to explore the world of engineering at a very young age. In the time of pandemic as students are not provided with many chances of interaction or collaboration, we try to bring in a sense of group work in the projects.

Indriya 4.0 will be conducted in 2 phases:

Ideation Phase :

  • ● Develop an idea based on any one of the given problem statements.

  • ● After successfully completing the registration procedure, draft an abstract of your project and submit when asked. Submission dates will be notified.

  • ● Send in your entries according to the instructions given via e-mail.

  • ● Selected projects will be qualified for the next phase.

Project Development Phase :

Selected projects will be assigned mentors who will help you in completing the project by providing resources and guidance

A detailed template will be provided which should be updated on a weekly basis, enabling the mentors to track your progress.

Participants are expected to complete the project by the end of June since the tentative dates for the project presentation could change with the social situation.

Problem statements :

  • Track 01 : Sustainable Development Goals(SDG's)

    Any project coming under the 17 SDG's, can be chosen by the participants. The specific SDGs should be mentioned while submitting the abstract.
    Click to view the 17 SDG's

  • Track 02 : Covid Responses

    Create relevant projects that can help Covid Relief in any way. The projects can come under any domain related to the pandemic.

Rules & Regulations

Students are allowed to participate individually or as a team, with a maximum of 4 members.

The abstract should be submitted in the given template only.

Participants are expected to be cooperative with the mentors.

The projects will be evaluated on the basis of Ideation, Implementation, Social Relevance, Application, and Presentation.

The decision of the judging panel would be final.

Ideas and projects by the participants must be original. Unoriginal/copied ideas or projects may be disqualified.
